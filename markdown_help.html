Markdown is a form of formatted text which is readily readable and writable as plain text.

Emphasize text by surrounding it with asterisks (*) or underscores (_). Use one character for italics (e.g. _italics_) and two for bold (e.g. **bold**).

Separate paragraphs by blank lines, e.g. 

    paragraph 1

    paragraph 2

    paragraph 3
and force line breaks by ending a line with a space before the newline.

Include program code by surrounding it with backquotes `.

Create bullet lists by starting and ending them with blank lines and starting each bullet with an asterisk (*) or plus sign (+) at the beginning of the line.

Create numbered lists by starting and ending them with blank lines and starting each item with a number followed by a period. Markdown will ignore your actual numbers and increment automatically.

Create links by enclosing URLs in angle brackets (e.g. <http://beingmeta.com/> or as [text](url) or [text](url "title").

Insert images as ![text](image url) or ![text](image url "title").

Horizontal rules are created by a series of asterisks, dashes, or underscores by themselves on a line.

For more information: Markdown syntax from John Gruber, who invented Markdown; other shorter and longer cheatsheets; and a clever online editor/previewer. Search for “Markdown” on the web to find even more.
